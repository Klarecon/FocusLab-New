Logging step — merge 2 screens into 1

Three ways to collapse "Where does your time go?" (rough estimate) + "Let's zoom into the big ones" (detail) into a single screen, with the four waste-types folded in.

Why this matters: the two-screen logging flow was one of Oren's biggest rejections. Below are 3 distinct single-screen approaches — each is live (check rows, type hours, watch the total). Pick one at the bottom of each, leave notes, and hit Download my notes.
Option A · Type accordion — estimate & detail in one expand

The four waste-types are collapsible sections. Open one, tick the specific drains that apply, and type the hours right there. The type header keeps a running subtotal. No separate "rough estimate" page — your estimate is the sum of what you log.

Why it works

  • Teaches the 4 types by scanning — name the type, know the fix.
  • Collapsed by default = not overwhelming; open only what applies to you.
  • One pass: tick + hours together, no rough-then-detail round trip.

Trade-offs

  • A click to expand each type (one extra tap per section).
  • Hours live inside accordions, so the full picture isn't all on screen at once.
Engine: drop the rough-estimate pass entirely. Pareto ranks straight off the per-drain hours you enter (summed per type/category). Simpler than today's two-pass, not riskier.

Option C · Guided one-pass — pick areas, then detail my pick

Keeps the smart "narrow then detail" logic of the two screens, but on one page. First tap the areas that hit you (chips, grouped by type). Only those expand inline with their drains + hours — no second page, and you never face the full firehose.

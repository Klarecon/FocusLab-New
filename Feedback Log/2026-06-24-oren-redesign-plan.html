The Next Version — Redesign Plan

Built from Oren's 23 June feedback · everything here is a proposal; nothing is live

The one thesis

Oren's verdict: the tool doesn't sell yet because it "makes me work." Too tabular, too much reading, methodology followed so literally it still feels like the 40-minute exercise.

The redesign has one job: deliver the promise — "free up 10 hours, in the next 3 minutes" — as a fast, plain-English, effortless experience that sells itself. Every change below serves that.

P0 blocks "it sells" P1 major friction P2 polish ◆ component uses a new Magic UI component

A · The tool experience (Oren's #1 priority)

Make it fast, plain, and effortless. This is what "sells the tool."

C · Needs your / Oren's call — not mine to decide

Oren was explicit: "If you decide to change the methodology, you discuss… or give me two options based on my methodology." These are flagged, not built, until decided:

Hours vs %: he questioned hours-per-week vs percentages. He can "live with" hours but wants it discussed. → I'll prepare 2 options on his methodology, not switch unilaterally.
Knowledge-workers scope: "we don't limit the tool to knowledge workers." → remove the qualifier (agreed), but broadening to other worker types is a discussion.
Combine the two intake steps into one: he agreed in principle ("only focus on A-level"). Confirm the exact merged flow before building.
Drop the over-estimate "validation" entirely? vs keep as inline nudge. Methodology-adjacent — confirm.
"It shouldn't have rendered a graph with three reasons… the language is incorrect" — I couldn't pin down which screen/graph he meant from the transcript. → One question for Oren to identify it before I fix the wrong thing.

E · Process (Oren's working-style notes)

Not UI, but he was clear, so it shapes how we work:

  • Finish the product before marketing. "Until you sell me the tool, everything else is a waste." → marketing plan is paused until the tool is approved.
  • Don't drift to "waste" when stuck. "The moment you get stuck, you go do waste stuff." → stay on the product.
  • Never change methodology unilaterally. Discuss, or bring 2 options on his methodology.
  • Nothing should appear he didn't ask for (e.g. the rogue "delegate"). Higher quality bar — he's showing investors.

Build sequence (after you approve the mockup)

  1. Confirm the decisions in section C (hours/%, merged steps) — quick, blocks the rest.
  2. Landing hero + value prop (P0 copy + particles + circular gauge) — the "it sells" front door.
  3. Analyzer plain-language + fix-in-place + same-page scoring + add-category + back buttons (P0 friction).
  4. Action Plan de-tabled → bento + circular reclaim gauge + suggested owners/due dates (P0/P1).
  5. Matrix overlap fix, selected-state contrast, blank-state clarity (P1).
  6. Copy/polish: White Elephant verb, CEO salary, stray emoji, "134 fixes", cross-browser emoji (P2).

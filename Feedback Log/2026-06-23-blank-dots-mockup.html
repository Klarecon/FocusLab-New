What blank means for the logic

The honest trade-off

  • Quadrant & quick-win count: a fix with no rating can't be placed. It shows an unrated state and is excluded from the "N quick wins" summary until rated — instead of silently counting as medium.
  • Sorting: the Action Plan sorts by impact. Unrated fixes drop to a "Needs rating" group at the bottom so they're not lost, then sort in once rated.
  • No data loss: this is purely the starting value. The store already holds per-fix scores — blank just means "unset" instead of defaulting to 2. SCORE_FROM_LEVEL (2/3/4) is untouched.

Why blank is better

  • Kills anchoring — no "everything's medium" garbage data
  • Forces a deliberate call → the matrix actually means something
  • Empty dots clearly signal "your turn"

The cost

  • More taps — user must rate every fix they keep
  • Needs an "unrated" empty state everywhere a quadrant shows
  • If they skip rating, the Impact Matrix has gaps until filled
My recommendation: go blank — your instinct is right. Pre-filled "2" produces lazy, anchored data and a Matrix that looks decided when it isn't. Blank costs a few taps but makes every quadrant placement earned. I'll add the unrated quadrant state + a "Needs rating" sort group so nothing breaks when a fix isn't scored yet. If you'd rather keep friction low, the fallback is pre-fill Effort only and leave Impact blank — but I'd do full-blank.

A The Money Path

Sequencing rule: anything that needs Oren's input is scheduled last, so the build never stalls on him. The one Oren handoff on the critical path is connecting his live payment account (A2) — built and tested in sandbox throughout, his account plugged in as the final pre-launch step. Worth giving him a heads-up early so it's ready when we reach it.
A1Accounts + saved plansthe foundation everything compounds on
  • Add sign-in (magic-link or Google) via a managed provider — Clerk or Supabase Auth — so you're not building auth from scratch.
  • Move saved plans from the browser to the user's account. This makes someone's plan theirs forever — it survives any device, any day — which is what justifies a subscription and builds the switching cost in your moat.
A2Billing + paywallturn love into revenue in one click
  • Use a merchant-of-record — Lemon Squeezy or Paddle — so taking payment and handling worldwide tax happen in one move. PH brings global traffic on day one; this welcomes it cleanly without a tax project.
  • The live payment account is Oren's (it's his account / business entity that receives the money). So I build and fully test the entire flow against a sandbox / test account — the build never waits on him. Plugging in Oren's live account is a 30-minute final step, done right before green-light.
  • Wire Free / $29-mo / $199-yr. (Coach $149 stays ready to switch on when Oren lands his first prepays.)
  • Place the paywall after Results, before the Focus Table — the spot you already designed, right where the value is most felt.
  • The one test that means you're ready: a stranger pays and instantly unlocks their Focus Table. That's your green-light.
A3The return loopwhat makes it compound
  • Ship one genuine reason to come back: a monthly re-assessment + a before/after progress view. This is the rung where retention starts working for you — each month a paying user re-runs it, the product proves its value again.
  • Minimal version is enough to launch; it can grow into the fuller dashboard later.
A4Trust + polish that ships with launchland on a real product
  • Privacy policy + terms + a friendly consent notice, live the same day email capture is — so every international signup is handled right from the first one (the MoR provider covers the tax/refund side for you).
  • A one-page source for every benchmark, so the aha-number is rock-solid. Frame it as "based on your inputs vs. role benchmarks" — confident and defensible.
  • Funnel analytics (visit → start → complete → email → pay) so you can see your wins land and double down on what converts.
  • Connect your custom domain — off the vercel.app alias.

B Launch asset prep — start now

Product Hunt

Lead with the aha-moment

  • Your Pareto result is genuinely screenshot-able — the gallery & hero GIF should be that moment, not a feature tour.
  • Build: tagline + one-liner, 4–6 gallery images, a 30–60s demo, your "maker's first comment," logo/thumbnail.
  • Warm the runway: a "notify me on launch" list, 15–20 people excited to engage in hour one, a Tue–Thu launch day.
  • PH delivers a big day — the win is converting that surge into a lasting email list + backlinks, so the email-capture path just needs to be smooth.
Reddit

Lead with the pain you understand

  • Your reddit-pain-miner work gives you the real words people use about time-waste — write to that and it lands as recognition.
  • Map your subreddits and post the way each community welcomes — value first, tool as a soft mention or in a comment.
  • Draft 2–3 genuinely useful posts; participate authentically beforehand so you show up as a member, not a marketer.

✓ The green-light

A and B run concurrently. You launch the week these are all true:

  • A stranger can pay and instantly unlock the Focus Table
  • Saved plans persist to the account across devices
  • The monthly return loop is live
  • Privacy + terms pages are up alongside email capture
  • Funnel analytics are firing
  • The custom domain is connected
Keep momentum by holding the line on scope. Anything not on this list is a great post-launch win. Shipping lean and launching into real users beats a longer polish, every time.

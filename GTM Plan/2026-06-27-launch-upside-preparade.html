00 How to read this

A pre-parade is the opposite exercise to the pre-mortem: assume you won, then name the causes. The discipline is the same — no cheerleading. Every asset below is something you can point at today, and every scenario is a plausible chain, not a fantasy.

The honest frame: you start with two things most pre-revenue founders don't have — a real, working product that's already live, and genuine domain authority (Oren's methodology + network). Most startups die before they have either. You're past that line. The whole game now is converting those two assets into a money path before momentum fades.

01 The three ways it wins

Each mirrors one of the three deaths from the pre-mortem — the same fork, walked the good way.

Win A — Resonance

The aha-moment travels

"You waste 14 hours a week" is screenshot-able. One Reddit thread or one creator's video where the diagnosis lands emotionally does what no ad budget can — people share the feeling, not the feature. The free tool becomes its own distribution.

Win B — Conversion

The fix is worth paying for

The free diagnosis makes the problem felt; the paid plan offers relief from a pain you just created. When the gate is placed right, paying isn't a leap — it's the obvious next step. The two halves work as one funnel.

Win C — Compounding

Coaches turn you into a system

One coach who adopts FocusLab brings a roster of clients and recurring usage. Seven of them = $1K MRR with no ad spend. Their authority teaches the category for you, and their clients become your individual users.

02 The assets you actually hold

What's genuinely in your favor — tagged have now (real today) vs to build (a lever you can pull). No vanity items.

A · Product & positioning
Have nowA finished, working product — not a deck

The analyzer, Focus Table, EVI Matrix and Payoff are built, tested (336 passing), and live. You can put a real thing in front of a real person today. That collapses the riskiest phase most founders are stuck in.

Why it's an edgeYou can validate with usage, not promises. Every conversation is a demo, not a pitch.
How to press itGet it in front of 10 real managers this month and watch them use it. Live product → live feedback loop is your unfair advantage.
Have nowA sharp, defensible position

"Not a tracker, not a quiz — a diagnosis with a ranked fix, in 15 minutes not 4 weeks." Your own competitor map confirms there's no exact twin. The closest matches are thin lead-magnet quizzes. That's a real opening, not a crowded fight.

Why it's an edgeA one-line frame an investor or a stranger can repeat is rare. You have it.
How to press itSay it the same way every time, everywhere. Repetition is what makes a category yours.
Have nowA free hook with a genuine emotional payload

Most "free tiers" are watered-down trials. Yours delivers the single most shareable moment — the gut-punch number — for $0, on a static site that costs almost nothing to run. The best salesperson you could build, working 24/7 at near-zero marginal cost.

Why it's an edgeLow cost-to-serve means you can give the hook away forever and still have healthy margins on Pro.
How to press itMake the result more shareable — a clean result card someone wants to post. Turn the aha into a referral engine.
B · Distribution & credibility
Have nowOren — methodology authority + a warm coach channel

The Pareto/Focus methodology isn't generic productivity advice — it's an expert's system, which answers the "why should I trust this?" question for free. And his network is a warm door into the exact segment (coaches) that converts fastest: 7 coaches = $1K MRR.

Why it's an edgeCredibility and distribution in one relationship. Most founders have to manufacture both from cold.

C · Economics & moat (the compounding kind)
To buildA moat that grows while you sleep

Three compounding assets, each stronger with scale: (1) accumulated waste-pattern data across roles/industries — a clone can copy the UI but not the dataset; (2) coach network effects; (3) switching costs from saved plans & progress history. None exist on day one — all are byproducts of usage.

Why it's an edgeThe longer you run, the harder you are to displace — the opposite of a feature that's easy to bolt on.
How to press itBuild the account/data layer (the pre-mortem's #1 blocker) — it's also what turns usage into a moat. Same investment, two payoffs.
Have nowCheap to run, capital-efficient by design

Static site, no infra bills, built by one person with AI leverage. Your burn is basically your time. That means you can take the slow, real path (let the category teach itself, let coaches compound) without a clock forcing a premature scale-up.

Why it's an edgePatience is a strategy you can actually afford. Even modest MRR is real profit, not a runway extension.
How to press itDon't spend into paid ads until unit economics close. Let free channels + coaches carry you — you have the luxury of waiting.

03 What "going right" looks like, step by step

The realistic compounding path — not a hockey stick, a staircase. Each step funds and de-risks the next.

  1. Money path closes. A stranger can pay and keep their plan. Now every visitor is a potential customer, not a dead end. (This is the unlock everything else waits on.)
  2. First 10 paying individuals. New managers who hit the aha-moment and want the fix. Proof the funnel converts at all — and your first case studies.
  3. Three coaches prepay. The coach track flips from hope to channel. Each brings recurring usage and borrows you their authority.
  4. Retention loop bites. Monthly re-assessment + progress tracking make month-2 a renewal, not a goodbye. MRR stops leaking and starts stacking.
  5. The dataset starts talking. Enough audits to show "managers in your role typically waste X on Y" — turning your benchmarks from static to earned, deepening the moat.
  6. $1K MRR, then a fork. Either lean into coaches (network effects) or individuals (referral + content) — chosen on data, funded by revenue, on your timeline.

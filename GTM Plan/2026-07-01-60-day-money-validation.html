Can This Make Money? A 60-Day Read

You don't have the luxury of a full retention cycle. So don't try to measure it. Here's the fastest honest test of whether FocusLab has money-making potential — with explicit GO / KILL thresholds you can read by end of August.

The whole thing in one line

In 6 weeks you can't measure retention — so measure conviction. Launch annual-first, run a $4k cold-ad test, and read whether strangers will bet upfront that this recurs.

All three agents landed independently on the same move: annual take-rate is your fastest, most honest signal. Someone paying a year upfront is voting — with money, today — that the value recurs. That vote resolves immediately; churn data doesn't arrive until Q4.

1 The reframe: conviction, not retention

The honest constraint: true churn and LTV need 3+ months. Your product launches mid-to-late July, so by end of August you have ~5–6 weeks of live selling — zero renewal cycles. Anyone quoting you a "retention %" in August is fabricating.

So the go/no-go can only rest on three things you can read fast:

  • Willingness to pay — do cold strangers pay at all, and at what funnel rate?
  • The money math — CAC vs. an assumed payback (LTV is stress-tested, not measured).
  • Conviction proxies — annual mix, unprompted return, and what buyers say to your face at the renewal-decision framing.
The mental shift: you are not proving the subscription works. You're proving people believe it will — enough to keep going. That's a defensible GO; "we've shown they stay" is not available to you yet, and claiming it would burn your credibility when real churn lands in Q4.

2 The one strategic move: annual-first

Every agent converged here without being told to. It's the answer to your exact 2-month problem.

🟡 Pricing
A monthly buyer can churn after one diagnosis and you won't know for 30–90 days — past your decision date. An annual buyer bets on 12 months today. Make annual the default toggle; monthly available.
🟣 Retention
Annual mix is the single most honest fast signal — a stated bet with real money, hard to fake. Launch a capped "Founding Member" annual with personal onboarding; the take-rate is the data.
🔘 Unit economics
Every annual sale ($199+) pays back CAC instantly. A 30%+ annual mix can rescue a marginal CAC — it's the lever that makes the money math survivable in a short window.
So: annual is the headline offer, framed as the obvious choice ("2 months free"). Keep monthly available for a clean "will anyone pay at all" read. Annual take-rate becomes your #1 go/no-go metric.

3 Price to learn faster — consider launching higher

The pricing agent's recommendation: launch at $39/mo · $290/yr, not $29/$199 — because in a short window, higher prices teach you faster.

  • People who pay $39 for a diagnosis-fixer really believe in it — that's the conviction signal you need, uncontaminated by impulse buys.
  • It's easy to discount down ("launch price, going up") and painful to raise on early customers. Start high, leave room to concede.
  • $29 sits in "impulse SaaS" territory where people buy without signaling real conviction — muddying exactly the read you want.
  • Don't go below $29 — cheap prices generate vanity conversions that prove nothing about durable value.
Decision for you + Oren: this changes the $29/$199 hypothesis in the OKR doc. My take: launch $39/$290 annual-first, and drop to $29 in week 3 only if conversion is dead and interviews say "too expensive" (not "don't need it"). Don't A/B three prices in the funnel — at your volume (~30–80 checkouts) that's noise, not signal.

4 The test: $4k of cold ads over 21 days

This is the money-math read. Real spend, staged — not the Product Hunt/Reddit launch, which is a separate, non-repeatable event.

1
Spend $4,000 over 21 days (~$150–200/day, split Meta + Google Search) → ~2,000 cold visitors at ~$2 CPC. Run 21 days, not 7 — you need weekday/weekend cycles and time for free users to return and buy.
2
Quarantine your traffic (non-negotiable). Cold paid ads in a separate UTM bucket from Product Hunt / Reddit / your own network. Only the cold-paid bucket counts for the money math — PH spikes convert 3–10× and will fool you into thinking you have a business you don't.
3
Kill gate at Day 5 / ~$1,000. If cold strangers won't even hand over an email to try a free tool (< ~6% capture on the first ~500 clicks), willingness-to-pay is dead at the top of the funnel. Stop — don't spend the other $3k.
4
Interview every buyer AND every canceller. 15-min calls. Buyers: "why'd you pay, do you keep it at renewal?" Cancellers tell you the truth buyers won't. This is your highest-yield activity at launch volume.
5
Force a 2nd session inside the window. Don't let the "monthly" loop be monthly for cohort #1 — schedule a 1-week check-in re-assessment so you observe before/after behavior inside the 6 weeks instead of after it. Instrument organic vs. prompted return separately.
The honest catch: at ~0.5% cold conversion, $4k yields only ~10 paid customers — enough to see if it works at all and compute a rough CAC, but not a statistically tight conversion rate (that needs $8–12k). Let the email-capture rate (~300 events) carry the statistical weight; treat the paid number as a directional pass/fail. Don't spend the extra $8k yet.

GO = money math green (conversion ≥0.5%, CAC <$60) AND conviction green (annual ≥40%, some organic return). "Recurring value is real, price is roughly right — keep going."
KILL / PIVOT = CAC >$120 or near-zero conversion → not a paid-acquisition business. OR annual <20% + "don't know what I'd do monthly" → it's a great report, and reports don't recur. That's a product/wrapper pivot (one-time fee? deepen the loop?), not a marketing fix.
The murky middle (annual 20–40%, CAC $60–120, decent depth): read as "recurring value plausible for a subset." Don't kill — narrow the ICP to whoever took annual and returned, and re-test that segment. "Plausible for consultants, one-time for managers" is a far more fundable finding than a blended average.

6 What you honestly cannot answer in 60 days — tell Oren plainly

  • True churn / renewal rate. Nobody will have hit month 3. Zero renewal data. This is a Q4 read.
  • LTV, payback, real unit economics. All depend on churn you can't see — they stay assumptions.
  • Whether the habit is durable. Week-4 re-engagement doesn't prove month-6.
  • Whether they'd renew without your white-glove founder attention. Founding-member hand-holding doesn't survive scale.
The exact line to give Oren: "By end of August I can tell you whether people believe it's worth subscribing — measured by what they pay upfront, whether they return on their own, and what they say at the renewal framing. I cannot tell you whether they actually stay. That's a Q4 read. This is a conviction check, not a retention proof — a green light to keep going, not evidence the subscription works."

Who we're really up against — and why most of them aren't us

A competitive map for the deck. The headline: FocusLab is not a time tracker and not a calendar tool. It's a self-input diagnostic — answer questions for 15 minutes, walk away with a ranked action plan. That narrow definition changes who actually competes with us.

Prepared 22 June 2026 · sources at the bottom

The one-line frame for investors 🫠

Trackers take four weeks to tell you the problem. Schedulers rearrange your calendar but never name the waste. Quizzes give you a personality type. FocusLab diagnoses the vital few sources of waste and hands you a ranked fix — in 15 minutes, one sitting.

Ring 1 — Direct competitors

Same job · diagnose → prioritize → plan · fast · no tracking

These ask the user some questions and hand back a personalized plan — the same click we're fighting for. None is a true twin. They're mostly lead-magnet quizzes bolted onto a coaching or SaaS brand: shallow output, no real Pareto engine, no impact/effort ranking. That gap is our opening.

Ring 2 — Adjacent

Same pain, different method · our pitch attacks these head-on

These are what an investor will name the moment they hear "where does my time go." We need a crisp answer for why we're not them.

Time trackers & analytics
RescueTime · Rize · Timely · Toggl · Clockify · Hubstaff · Time Doctor
AI calendar & scheduling
Reclaim.ai · Motion · Clockwise · Morgen · Akiflow
  • Trackers: passively measure where time goes. Our "15 minutes, not four weeks" line is aimed straight at them — they reveal the problem after weeks of data and never hand you a fix.
  • Schedulers: optimize time (block focus, auto-schedule). They never diagnose root-cause waste or produce a "stop doing X" plan.

Ring 3 — Substitutes

What managers use today instead of any tool

The status quo is our biggest real competitor — not another app.

  • Coaching platforms: BetterUp Manage, Waggle, Cloverleaf — the premium, ongoing, expensive version of "help me lead better."
  • DIY: a spreadsheet time audit, the Eisenhower matrix, a Pareto template in Asana/Notion, or a one-off consultant. "Just do it yourself" is what we have to beat.

The strategic read

  1. No exact twin. Closest matches (BeeProductive, The ONE Thing) are thin lead-magnet quizzes, not products. Green field — but we'll have to teach the category.
  2. Our edge is the Pareto engine + ranked action plan. Quizzes give vibes; trackers give data; we give a prioritized fix list.
  3. Investors will pattern-match us to RescueTime / Reclaim. Pre-empt it: "We're not a tracker. Trackers take four weeks to tell you the problem. We tell you the fix in 15 minutes."
  4. Watch the incumbents. Reclaim, Motion, or ClickUp could bolt a "15-min diagnostic" onto existing distribution. Speed and depth-of-methodology are our defense.

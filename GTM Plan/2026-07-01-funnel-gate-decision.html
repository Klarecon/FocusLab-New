Where the Gate Goes

Deciding what's free, what asks for an email, and what asks for money — before we build the money path. Three specialist agents weighed in. Here's the call.

FocusLab · 2026-07-01 · decision-first, no code written

The one-line answer

The mid-funnel gate is an email, never a payment. Money only ever lands on the Focus Table.

Your instinct to A/B test is right — but the axis to test is where the email ask sits, not "email vs payment." Payment-gating a half-finished diagnosis to cold ad traffic is the one option all three agents would kill before it burns a dollar.

1 The question you asked

When we split ad traffic to test funnels, Variant A shows only a partial Pareto as a lead magnet and gates the rest. The open question was: in Variant A, is that mid-gate an email capture or a payment? And is partial-Pareto even a good idea versus giving the full Pareto free (Variant B)?

Killed first: "partial results → pay $29 to see the rest." To a stranger who clicked an ad 90 seconds ago, that reads as a bait-and-switch. It spikes bounce, and Meta/Google penalise a bad post-click experience with higher costs — so it literally raises your cost per customer. First-session cash conversion for a $29 tool from cold traffic is ~0.2–1%. You don't win on click one.

2 What all three agents agreed on

✓
The gate is an email, not a payment. The only thing a cold visitor should be asked for mid-funnel is a cheap "yes" that costs them nothing and lets you keep selling after they leave.
✓
Payment belongs on the Focus Table — the cure, not the diagnosis. Free tells you where the waste is; paid fixes it. That's an honest line a cold user can feel.
✓
The aha must land before you ask for anything. Even in Variant A you never gate the raw "I'm losing 9 hrs/week" number — you give that free, then gate the depth.
✓
One email, captured once, reused everywhere. The same email is the magic-link account and the Lemon Squeezy checkout. Ask three times and you lose on friction alone.
✓
Judge on 30-day blended conversion + CAC — not day-1 checkout rate, which would falsely flatter the "more free" variant and hide the email list's value.
🟢 Product Manager
Email, decisively. Run A — but only the version that gives the aha headline free and email-gates the depth. That's A's whole edge: a retargeting asset B builds later.
🟡 Growth / Acquisition
Email, never payment. Expects B to win revenue-per-click: full aha builds trust, email-to-save still captures the lead, paywall the fix.
🟢 Value-Perception
A half-diagnosis aborts the aha and poisons reciprocity. Bet on B. If you must gate A, email only — and note that concedes the Pareto is a lead magnet.

4 How to judge the test

MetricWhy it's the one that matters
30-day blended visitor→paidThe decision metric. Counts day-0 buyers and nurtured email conversions, so A's delayed payoff is measured fairly.
CAC + payback periodNormalises for spend and traffic quality — tells you which funnel is actually buyable at scale. Watch whether gating inflates CPC via ad-quality penalties.
Email capture rate × email→paidQuantifies the list asset. If a variant wins because of the nurture list, that's the thesis confirmed.
The trap: don't judge on first-session checkout rate. It flatters the "more free" variant and throws away the entire value of the email list — which is how you recoup ad spend on cold traffic.

5 Recommendation

Drop "payment after partial" from the test entirely. Run the A/B as email-gate placement — Variant A gates the depth of the Pareto, Variant B gives the full Pareto and gates the Focus Table. Payment only ever lands on the Focus Table. Build the movable gate once; let the ads decide.

What this unblocks for the build

  • Workstream A1/A2 can proceed without picking A or B first — the paywall is a config line, not two separate builds.
  • The one plumbing rule to get right: one email, captured once, threaded through magic-link auth and Lemon Squeezy checkout. Get that wrong and you'll hand one variant an unfair win on friction.
  • Analytics (funnel tracking, already on the A4 list) is now a launch requirement, not a nice-to-have — the A/B test is worthless without it.

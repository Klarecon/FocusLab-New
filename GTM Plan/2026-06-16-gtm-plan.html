Individual Track

VALIDATE FIRST

Why This Pricing

Free tier: The Pareto Analyzer delivers the "aha" moment: "You waste 14 hours a week, and 80% comes from just 3 sources." That emotional hit is the hook. The user learned what's wrong — now they need how to fix it. That's the paid gate.

$29/mo: Impulse-purchase range for a working professional. Competitors (Rize, Reclaim, Sunsama) charge $10-30/mo for passive tracking. We charge $29 because we deliver more — not just data, but a complete action plan. Room to test $19 or $39 later with data.

$149/mo: A coach charges $200-500/hr. FocusLab replaces 1+ hours of manual diagnostic work per client. ROI is obvious: spend $149/mo, save 5-10 hours/mo across clients. Only gets built if validation confirms demand.

Part 8 What You Need to Learn

Five concepts, each a 30-minute learning session. Understand these well enough to explain them to Oren.

1

Freemium as Lead Generation

What it means: Giving away part of your product isn't losing money — it's marketing. The free tier creates an emotional moment ("I waste HOW much time?!") that makes people want to pay for the solution. Same model as Canva (free designs, pay for premium) and Calendly (free scheduling, pay for team features).
Defend it like this: "The free tool costs us nothing to run — it's static, no server costs — but it does the job of a sales team. It shows people their problem and makes them want the fix."
2

Validation Before Building

What it means: Don't build features for a customer segment until you've confirmed they actually want it. Talk to real people first. 10-15 conversations cost nothing and take 2 weeks. Building a coach dashboard costs weeks of engineering and might be wasted.
Defend it like this: "We're not guessing — we're asking. If 6 out of 15 coaches say yes, we build. If they say no, we saved ourselves weeks of wasted work."
3

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

What it means: How much you spend to get one paying customer. If you spend $300/mo on ads and get 10 users, your CAC is $30. Rule of thumb: Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) should be at least 3x your CAC. At $29/mo with 6-month retention, LTV = $174, so CAC should be under $58.
Defend it like this: "I know exactly what each customer costs to acquire, and I'm tracking it weekly. Our target is under $50 CAC, which gives us healthy margins at $29/mo."
4

The Funnel

What it means: The journey from stranger to paying customer. Each stage has a conversion rate. The job of marketing is to improve those rates.
Stranger sees ad / blog post / LinkedIn post Click-through: 1-3%
Visitor lands on FocusLab Start rate: 30-50%
Starts free Pareto Analyzer Completion: 40-60%
Sees waste results → gives email Email capture: 30%+
Pays $29/mo for Focus Table Conversion: 5-8%
Defend it like this: "I can tell you exactly where people drop off and what we're doing about each drop-off point."
5

Why Two Tracks, Not One

What it means: Running coach outreach AND content marketing simultaneously. Coaches give you fast revenue through Oren's network (weeks). Content builds a growing base that compounds (months). Neither alone hits targets, but together they cover both speed and scale.
Defend it like this: "Coaches give us revenue in weeks. Content gives us revenue that grows every month. Neither one alone hits our targets, but together they cover both speed and scale."

Part 9 Q&A Defense Guide

The questions Oren (or anyone) is likely to ask, and how to answer with confidence. Click to expand.

"Why give the Pareto Analyzer away for free? That's our best feature."
It IS our best feature — and that's exactly why it's free. The Pareto Analyzer creates the problem awareness that makes people pay. Without it, we're selling a fix to people who don't know they're broken. With it, every free user is a qualified lead who already knows they waste X hours per week. The analyzer costs us nothing to run — there are no server costs, no per-user costs. It's the best salesperson we could build.
"Why $29/mo? Why not $49? Why not $9?"
$29 is in the "impulse purchase" range for a working professional — low enough that they don't need manager approval, high enough that it signals real value. Competitors like Rize and Reclaim charge $10-25/mo for passive tracking. We charge $29 because we deliver more — not just data, but a complete action plan. We can always test $19 or $39 later once we have conversion data. Starting at $29 gives us room to move in either direction.
"How do you know coaches will pay $149/mo?"
Right now, I don't — and that's the point of the validation step. Before we build anything for coaches, you'll talk to 10-15 of them and ask directly. If 6+ say yes enthusiastically, we build it. If they're lukewarm, we skip it and focus on individual users. We're not gambling — we're testing a hypothesis before we invest.
"Why not go straight to companies? That's where the real money is."
Companies pay more per deal, but they take 3-6 months to close. We need revenue in weeks, not quarters. We'll absolutely pursue L&D and HR teams starting month 4, but right now we don't have case studies, testimonials, or social proof — all things enterprise buyers require. The first 90 days build that proof with coaches and individuals so we have ammunition for bigger deals later.
"$300/mo on Facebook ads — isn't that a waste?"
We're not spending $300 blindly. We start at $5/day with 3-4 different ad creatives. Within a week, the data tells us which ones work. We kill the losers and scale the winners. At $0.20-0.30 per click, $300/mo gets us 1,000-1,500 people trying the free tool. If even 5% convert at $29/mo, that's $1,450-2,175 in monthly revenue from a $300 investment. That's a 4-7x return. But we'll know within 2 weeks if the math works — if it doesn't, we cut the spend and redirect.
"What if nobody pays? What if the conversion rate is 0%?"
Then we learn something critical: either the free-to-paid gate is in the wrong place, the price is wrong, or the Focus Table doesn't deliver enough value. Each has a fix. We can move the gate earlier or later, test different prices, or improve the Focus Table based on what free users say they're missing. Zero conversion isn't failure — it's data that tells us exactly what to fix. What WOULD be failure is spending 6 months building features nobody asked for.
"What makes FocusLab different from just using a spreadsheet?"
Three things. First, the methodology — this isn't just a time log, it's a Pareto analysis designed by an expert that surfaces the 20% of activities causing 80% of waste. You can't get that from a spreadsheet unless you know the methodology. Second, the fix engine — the Focus Table matches problems with prioritized solutions, scores impact vs effort, and produces an action plan. Third, speed — a spreadsheet-based time audit takes weeks. FocusLab takes 15 minutes.
"Why are you the right person to sell this?"
I know content marketing and I know how to create compelling ads. That covers the two channels we're starting with. I also built the product, so I understand every feature and can write about it authentically. What I don't know yet — pricing optimization, sales processes, enterprise deals — I'm learning actively and we're starting with channels that don't require those skills. By the time we're ready for enterprise sales in month 4+, I'll have the data and experience to handle it.
"What happens if the coach track fails validation?"
Nothing bad. We spent 2 weeks and zero dollars learning that coaches aren't our market. The individual Pro track runs regardless — it's not dependent on coaches. We take the $149/mo engineering effort we saved and put it into improving conversion on the individual track: better onboarding, more content, optimized ads. We're not betting the whole plan on coaches — they're an accelerator, not the foundation.
"How do you know $1K MRR is realistic in 90 days?"
It requires 35 Pro users at $29/mo, or 7 coaches at $149/mo, or some combination. With 1,000+ free tool users/month (from ads + content), a 5% conversion rate gives us 50 paying users — more than enough. The coach track adds to that if it validates. I'm being conservative — the bigger risk is that conversion is lower than 5%, which is why we're tracking it weekly and optimizing.
"What's the long-term play? You can't stay at $1K MRR forever."
The first 90 days prove the model works. Once we have paying customers, case studies, and conversion data, we unlock bigger moves: L&D team sales ($499/mo+), annual contracts, content that's ranking in Google, and a coach network that grows itself. $1K MRR is proof of concept. The same playbook scales — more content, more ad budget, more coaches, bigger contracts. But we're not planning for month 12 until month 3 proves month 1 was right.

Part 10 Success Criteria

Clear signals for what "working," "needs adjustment," and "abandon" look like.

The Plan is WORKING

By week 12, all of these are true:

  • 200+ free completions/month
  • 30%+ email capture rate
  • 5%+ free-to-paid conversion
  • $1K MRR (Pro + Coach)
  • At least 1 case study
  • Facebook ads ROAS > 1x

NEEDS Adjustment

If any of these are true:

  • Completions under 50/mo → traffic problem
  • Email capture under 15% → engagement problem
  • Conversion under 2% → value/pricing problem
  • Coach validation fails → skip Coach tier
  • Ad ROAS under 0.5x after 4 weeks → wrong audience or creative

ABANDON or Pivot

If after 12 weeks + optimizations:

  • Fewer than 10 paying users total
  • Nobody completes the free tool (product problem)
  • CAC exceeds LTV consistently

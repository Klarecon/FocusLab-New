5 Retention (Why They Stay Past Month 1)

Biggest risk: FocusLab feels like a one-session tool. Three features fix this:

  • Monthly re-assessment: "It's been 30 days — has your waste changed?" Waste patterns shift as projects change.
  • Progress tracking: Weekly nudges on action plan deadlines. Turns one-shot plan into accountability partner.
  • Before/after dashboard: "You've identified 47 hours of waste, acted on 31." This is the reason to stay subscribed.
Say it like this "We're not selling a one-time audit. Waste patterns change every month — new projects, shifting teams. The re-assessment keeps it relevant. The progress tracker makes us an accountability partner. That's the difference between a tool you use once and one you rely on."
